Launch of LadyLabs
Innovation Hub.

Featuring former Minister of Education Obiageli Ezekwesili, the launch marks a new chapter in empowering female tech founders.

Published on October 12, 2024

The Pearls Africa Youth Foundation recently celebrated a monumental milestone with the official launch of the LadyLabs Innovation Hub in Lagos. The event was graced by the esteemed presence of Obiageli Ezekwesili, former Minister of Education and a staunch advocate for youth and women's empowerment, who delivered a powerful keynote address.

LadyLabs is designed to be more than just a co-working space; it's a sanctuary for innovation, collaboration, and growth tailored specifically for women in technology. It provides a supportive ecosystem where female developers, designers, and entrepreneurs can bring their ideas to life, access cutting-edge resources, and connect with industry mentors.

A Space Intentionally Designed for Women

Recognising the unique challenges faced by women in the tech industry, LadyLabs addresses the critical need for safe, inclusive spaces. Many existing hubs inadvertently foster environments that can be intimidating or exclusive. LadyLabs counters this by prioritizing a culture of mutual support and shared knowledge.

The hub is equipped with high-speed internet, modern workstations, meeting rooms, and dedicated areas for workshops and coding bootcamps. But more importantly, it's equipped with a community that understands the shared journey of women navigating the tech landscape.

The Vision for the Future

During her address, Dr. Ezekwesili emphasized the transformative power of technology in driving Africa's economic development and the indispensable role of women in that narrative. "When you equip a woman with technical skills, you are not just empowering an individual; you are uplifting an entire community," she stated, echoing Pearls Africa's core philosophy.

The launch of LadyLabs represents a significant step forward in our mission to achieve proportional representation of women in technical leadership roles across Africa. We envision this space as the birthplace of countless groundbreaking startups and the launchpad for the next generation of female tech titans.
