Transforming Learning,
Empowering Africa.

A landmark workshop in partnership with Ashoka Africa focused on transforming education and empowerment across the continent.

Published on September 28, 2024

In a significant stride towards educational reform, Pearls Africa Youth Foundation recently partnered with Ashoka Africa to host the "Transforming Learning, Empowering Africa" workshop. This collaborative effort brought together educators, policymakers, and social entrepreneurs to devise innovative strategies for the continent's educational landscape.

Ashoka Africa, known for its extensive network of leading social entrepreneurs, provided invaluable insights into scalable educational models. Together, we explored how cross-sector partnerships can address systemic barriers to learning and skill acquisition, particularly for marginalized communities.

Focusing on STEM and Experiential Learning

A central theme of the workshop was the urgent need to transition from rote memorization to experiential, hands-on learning paradigms with a strong emphasis on STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics).

Our sessions highlighted Pearls Africa's successful models, such as GirlsCoding, as practical examples of how targeted interventions can bridge the gender gap in tech. Discussions centered on how these models can be adapted and integrated into mainstream educational curricula across different African nations.

A Unified Vision for the Continent

"The challenges facing education in Africa are complex, but they are not insurmountable if we pool our resources and expertise," noted Beatrice Alao, Executive Director of Pearls Africa. "This workshop is a testament to the power of collective action."

The event concluded with a shared commitment to pilot newly developed learning frameworks in select communities over the coming year. This partnership marks the beginning of a sustained effort to ensure that youth across Africa have access to relevant, high-quality education that prepares them for the future workforce.
